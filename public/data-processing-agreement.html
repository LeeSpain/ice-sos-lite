Data Processing Agreement (GDPR Compliance)

Effective Date: January 29, 2025

GDPR Compliance Notice: This Data Processing Agreement outlines how ICE SOS Lite processes personal data in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 and other applicable privacy laws.

1. Definitions and Scope

1.1 Key Definitions:

  • Controller: ICE SOS Lite (the entity determining purposes and means of processing)
  • Data Subject: You (the individual whose personal data is processed)
  • Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
  • Processing: Any operation performed on personal data (collection, storage, use, etc.)
  • Special Categories: Health data, location data, biometric data

1.2 Processing Scope:

This agreement covers all personal data processing activities related to:

  • Emergency response services
  • User account management
  • Medical information storage
  • Location tracking and sharing
  • Communication with emergency contacts
  • Service analytics and improvement

2. Legal Basis for Processing

Data Category Legal Basis (GDPR Article 6) Special Category Basis (Article 9) Purpose
Account Information Consent (6.1.a) / Contract (6.1.b) N/A Service provision
Medical Data Vital Interests (6.1.d) Vital Interests (9.2.c) Emergency medical response
Location Data Consent (6.1.a) / Vital Interests (6.1.d) N/A Emergency location sharing
Emergency Contacts Consent (6.1.a) N/A Emergency notifications
Usage Analytics Legitimate Interests (6.1.f) N/A Service improvement

3. Data Categories and Processing Activities

3.1 Personal Data Categories:

Identity Data:

  • Name, email address, phone number
  • User ID and account credentials
  • Profile photos and preferences

Special Category Data (Enhanced Protection):

  • Health Data: Medical conditions, allergies, medications, blood type
  • Location Data: GPS coordinates, geofence data, location history
  • Biometric Data: Device authentication data (where applicable)

Contact Data:

  • Emergency contact information
  • Healthcare provider details
  • Family and carer contact information

Technical Data:

  • Device identifiers and specifications
  • Usage analytics and performance metrics
  • Security and audit logs

4. Processing Activities

4.1 Emergency Response Processing:

  • Purpose: Coordinate emergency assistance
  • Data: Location, medical information, emergency contacts
  • Recipients: Emergency services, medical responders, emergency contacts
  • Retention: 7 years for emergency records (legal requirement)
  • Legal Basis: Vital interests protection

4.2 Account Management Processing:

  • Purpose: Service delivery and user authentication
  • Data: Account credentials, profile information, preferences
  • Recipients: Internal systems, authenticated service providers
  • Retention: Duration of account plus 2 years
  • Legal Basis: Contract performance and consent

4.3 Analytics Processing:

  • Purpose: Service improvement and optimization
  • Data: Usage patterns, performance metrics (anonymized)
  • Recipients: Internal analytics team, approved analytics providers
  • Retention: 25 months (Google Analytics compliance)
  • Legal Basis: Legitimate interests

5. Data Subject Rights

Your Rights Under GDPR:

5.1 Right of Access (Article 15):

  • Request a copy of your personal data
  • Information about processing activities
  • Details about data recipients and retention

5.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16):

  • Correct inaccurate personal data
  • Complete incomplete data
  • Update medical information

5.3 Right to Erasure (Article 17):

  • Request deletion of personal data
  • Limitation: Emergency data may be retained for legal compliance
  • Medical data erasure may be limited for safety reasons

5.4 Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18):

  • Limit how we process your data
  • Suspend processing during disputes
  • Limitation: Cannot restrict emergency processing

5.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20):

  • Receive data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Transfer data to another service provider
  • Export emergency contact and medical information

5.6 Right to Object (Article 21):

  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Opt-out of direct marketing
  • Limitation: Cannot object to vital interest processing

6. Data Protection Measures

6.1 Technical Safeguards:

  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption for data at rest and in transit
  • Access Controls: Role-based access with multi-factor authentication
  • Audit Logging: Comprehensive logging of all data access
  • Data Minimization: Collect only necessary data for service provision
  • Pseudonymization: Where possible, data is pseudonymized

6.2 Organizational Safeguards:

  • Staff Training: Regular GDPR and privacy training
  • Privacy by Design: Privacy considerations in all system development
  • Data Protection Impact Assessments: Regular privacy impact assessments
  • Vendor Management: GDPR compliance requirements for all suppliers

7. International Data Transfers

7.1 Transfer Safeguards:

  • EU/EEA Processing: Primary data processing within EU/EEA
  • Adequacy Decisions: Transfers only to countries with adequacy decisions
  • Standard Contractual Clauses: Where adequacy decisions don't exist
  • Emergency Transfers: Vital interest transfers for emergency response

7.2 Emergency Service Transfers:

During emergencies, data may be transferred internationally to:

  • Emergency services in your location
  • Medical facilities providing care
  • Emergency contacts in other countries
  • International emergency response coordination centers

8. Data Retention Schedule

Data Type Retention Period Legal Basis Disposal Method
Account Data Account duration + 2 years Contract and legal obligations Secure deletion
Emergency Records 7 years Legal and regulatory requirements Secure archival then deletion
Medical Data Account duration + 5 years Healthcare regulations Secure deletion with audit trail
Location Data 90 days (rolling) Service necessity Automatic deletion
Analytics Data 25 months Business need and legal compliance Anonymization then deletion

9. Breach Notification

9.1 Data Breach Response:

  • Detection: Automated monitoring and manual reporting
  • Assessment: Risk evaluation within 24 hours
  • Authority Notification: Supervisory authority notification within 72 hours
  • Individual Notification: High-risk breaches notified within 72 hours
  • Remediation: Immediate action to contain and resolve breaches

9.2 Emergency Data Breach Protocol:

For breaches affecting emergency services:

  • Immediate notification to emergency service partners
  • Priority restoration of emergency response capabilities
  • Enhanced monitoring during recovery period
  • Detailed incident reporting to relevant authorities
